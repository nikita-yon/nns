Conventions

All modes: CW, SSB and modes for which activity centers are indicated, as well as AM. (When using AM, care must be taken not to interfere with stations in the adjacent channel)

Image transmission: Any method of image transmission - analog or digital - in which the signal has the appropriate bandwidth. For example, SSTV or FAX.

Narrowband modes: All modes having a signal bandwidth not exceeding 500 Hz. For example, CW, RTTY, PSK, etc.

Digital modes: Any digital modes that have an appropriate signal bandwidth. For example, RTTY, PSK, MT63, etc.

Notes

The frequencies in the plan are understood as signal frequencies and not as suppressed carrier frequencies. The entire signal band must fit within the allocated radio frequency band.

To prevent transmissions outside the allocated bands, the maximum frequency value on the Suppressed Carrier Tuning Indicator for USB (voice) mode should be 3 kHz below the top of the band on the 20 m to 10 m bands.

(*) minimum frequency value on the tuning indicator showing the suppressed carrier frequency for LSB (voice) mode : 1843, 3603 and 7053 kHz

Morse code (CW) QSOs are allowed in all radio frequency bands except for the bands reserved exclusively for radio beacons. (IARU Recommendation DV05_C4_Rec_13)

Amplitude modulation (AM) can be used in telephone sections (LSB, USB) provided it does not interfere with stations on adjacent channels. (NRRL Davos 05).

Using the sidebands

Below 10 MHz, the lower band (LSB) is used; above 10 MHz, the upper band (USB).

Competitions

If no DX traffic is involved, competitions must not be held in the bands 3500-3510 kHz and 3775-3800 kHz.

During major international competitions, non-participating radio amateurs are encouraged to use the WARC HF bands (30, 17 and 12m) . (DV05_C4_Rec_07)

Competitions must be limited to the 160, 80, 40, 20, 15 and 10m bands. The 60, 30, 17 and 12m bands must not be used for competition. (VIE16_C4_Rec_06)

See the IARU Region 1 HF Managers' Guide for further guidance on the RF bands used for competition.

radio beacons

Remote radio control

Radio remote control refers to the situation where a licensed radio operator controls the operation of an amateur radio station from a remote control terminal.

If the station is operating remotely, the following conditions apply:

The communications administration of the country in whose territory the remotely controlled amateur radio station is located must either allow or not object to such control.

The call sign used for remote operation must be issued by the communications administration of the country in whose territory the remote-controlled radio station is located. This rule applies regardless of the location of the operator.

It should be noted that CEPT T/R 61-01 only applies to radio operators using their own call sign with the appropriate country prefix, and only when the radio operator is actually visiting that country, and not for remote control.

Any additional requirements regarding the participation of remote controlled stations in competitions or award programs are subject to the regulation of their organizers. (SC11_C4_REC_07) and (VA14_C4_REC_04).
