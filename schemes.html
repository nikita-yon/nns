    • J antenna for amateurs


    description

    The J-pole antenna, faster than the J-antenna,
    is a vertical omnidirectional front antenna used in the shortwave frequency bands.
    It was mass-produced by Hans Beggerow in 1909 for use in the Zeppelin airships.
    Following the airship, he composed of one half-wave long-wave wire radiator,
    serial cascaded with a quarter-wave analytical stub of the transmission line,
    which matched the impedances with the antenna line. mounted vertically,
    giving it the shape of the letter "J", and by 1943 it was called the J-antenna.
